Annual Remembrance Gala Raises Record Funds for Memorial

Date: May 10, 2025 | Location: Grand Ballroom, London Convention Centre

The Brothers in Arms Memorial Foundation held its annual Remembrance Gala last weekend, bringing together over 300 community members, veterans, and supporters for an evening of celebration and fundraising. This year's event raised an unprecedented $175,000, exceeding last year's total by more than 35%.

The evening featured a keynote address from retired Lieutenant-General David Anderson, who spoke movingly about the significance of the memorial project and the bonds forged between Indian and Canadian soldiers throughout history. "This memorial isn't just about remembering the past," Anderson noted. "It's about building bridges in our present community that will extend into the future."

Special Performances

Attendees were treated to a series of cultural performances highlighting both Indian and Canadian heritage. The London Symphony Orchestra Quartet opened the evening with "The Maple Leaf Forever" before transitioning to traditional Indian compositions. Later, the Srishti Cultural Dance Group presented a specially choreographed piece representing the journey of soldiers from both nations.

"The performances tonight weren't just entertainment—they were living demonstrations of the cultural bridge this memorial represents. Seeing both traditions honored side by side was profoundly moving." — Anita Sharma, Gala Committee Chair

The Srishti Cultural Dance Group performing "Bridges of Memory" at the gala.

Silent Auction Success

One of the highlights of the evening was the silent auction, which featured donated items including:

  • A handcrafted replica of the memorial design signed by the architect
  • Vacation packages to both the Canadian Rockies and Kerala, India
  • Several original artworks depicting historical scenes of Indian and Canadian troops
  • Dinner with the Mayor and special guests from the Indian Consulate

The auction alone raised over $50,000, with the vacation packages being the most sought-after items of the evening.

Impact on Memorial Construction

The funds raised at the gala will directly support three key aspects of the memorial's construction:

  1. Completion of the central granite monument inscriptions
  2. Installation of informational plaques along the memorial pathway
  3. Creation of a digital archive kiosk that will allow visitors to search for specific soldiers' stories
