Key Battles & Events

2nd Battle of Ypres

(April 1915 – May 1915)

The first major gas attack on the Western Front, where German forces unleashed chlorine gas.

Indian and Canadian soldiers, including the Lahore Division, fought to close gaps in the Allied lines, preventing a full German breakthrough despite suffering heavy casualties.

Battle of St. Julien

(April 1915)

A key engagement during the 2nd Battle of Ypres. Indian and Canadian troops endured some of the war’s first poison gas attacks while defending their positions.

Their resistance helped slow the German advance and prevent the collapse of the Allied front.

Battle of Festubert

(May 1915)

A prolonged trench battle where Indian and Canadian soldiers launched night assaults against heavily fortified German defenses.

Despite heavy losses, their efforts secured strategic ground and demonstrated the bravery and resilience of colonial forces in the war.

Battle of the Somme

(July 1916 – November 1916)

One of the bloodiest battles of the war, with over a million casualties. Indian and Canadian forces played key roles, with the 20th Deccan Horse launching a rare cavalry charge at High Wood.

Despite heavy machine gun fire, their attack contributed to the eventual capture of the position.

Battle of Vimy Ridge

(April 1917)

A historic Canadian victory, where all four Canadian divisions fought together for the first time.

Sikh artillerymen of the Lahore Division supported the offensive, helping capture the ridge, which had resisted previous British and French attacks. The battle became a defining moment for Canada’s national identity.
